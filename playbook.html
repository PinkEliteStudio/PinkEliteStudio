2. Success Formula

2.1 The Formula

Scale your brand with: Revenue Growth = (SEO Traffic × Conversion Rate) + (Ad ROAS × Ad Spend) + (Email Revenue × Open Rate).

  • SEO Traffic: Drive 10k organic visits with 10 high-intent keywords (e.g., “wellness e-commerce marketing”) in 6 months.
  • Conversion Rate: Boost from 2% to 4% with optimized landing pages and trust signals (e.g., testimonials).
  • Ad ROAS: Achieve 4:1 ROAS (4x revenue per $1 spent) with Meta, Google, and TikTok ads.
  • Email Revenue: Generate 20% of revenue from email funnels with 30% open rates.

2.2 What is ROAS?

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) measures ad efficiency: ROAS = Revenue from Ads / Ad Spend. Example: $500 Meta Ads generate $2,000 sales → ROAS = $2,000 / $500 = 4:1. Target 4:1 ROAS to ensure $4 revenue per $1 spent, aligning with e-commerce benchmarks.

2.3 Ad Budget Guidelines

You’re responsible for ad costs (separate from the $4,995 playbook). Recommended budgets:

  • Starting Budget: $500/month ($120/week) for $100k–$250k revenue brands; $1,000/month ($240/week) for $250k–$500k brands.
  • Allocation: 50% Meta ($250), 30% Google ($150), 20% Pinterest/TikTok ($100).
  • Scaling: Increase 20% weekly for >4:1 ROAS ads (e.g., $250 → $300). Pause <2:1 ROAS ads.
  • Cap: Limit to 10–15% of monthly revenue (e.g., $833–$1,250 for $100k/year).
  • Expected ROAS: 3:1–5:1 after 3 months with optimization.

Example: A $200k brand spends $750/month ($375 Meta, $225 Google, $150 Pinterest/TikTok). With 4:1 ROAS, $750 generates $3,000 in sales, adding $27,000 in 6 months.

Case Study: A supplement brand spent $600/month on ads, optimized for 4:1 ROAS, and added $20k in sales in 4 months.

3. Discovery Process

3.1 Audit Current Performance

  • Create a Google Analytics account (analytics.google.com). Add tracking code to Shopify (Settings > Checkout > Additional Scripts).
  • Export data on traffic sources, bounce rates (aim <40%), and conversions (e.g., purchases).
  • Log into Meta/Google Ads to review cost-per-click ($1–$3 target), ROAS (2:1 minimum), and conversion rates (2%+).
  • Install Hotjar (hotjar.com, free plan) to analyze clicks and scrolls on product pages.
  • Document in the KPI Tracker.

3.2 Identify Pain Points

  • Conduct a SWOT analysis: List Strengths (e.g., strong products), Weaknesses (e.g., high cart abandonment), Opportunities (e.g., SEO), Threats (e.g., competitors).
  • Create a Typeform survey (typeform.com, free plan) asking: “What stops you from buying?” or “What do you value in wellness products?”
  • Map your funnel in Lucidchart (lucidchart.com, free trial) to identify drop-offs (e.g., 50% abandon cart).
  • List top 3 pain points (e.g., low email opens, poor SEO rankings).

3.3 Set SMART Goals

  • Define 3–5 Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound goals (e.g., “Increase ROAS to 4:1 in 90 days,” “Rank top 10 for 5 keywords in 6 months”).
  • Calculate customer lifetime value (CLV) in Shopify (Reports > Customers) to align goals (e.g., $200 CLV → prioritize repeat purchases).
  • Log goals in the KPI Tracker and review weekly via Google Sheets.

4. SEO for Wellness Brands

4.1 Why SEO Matters

SEO drives 30% of e-commerce traffic, reducing customer acquisition costs by 60%. For $100k+ brands, ranking for “organic supplements” or “wellness marketing” attracts high-intent buyers.

4.2 Backend Setup

  • Generate sitemap.xml in Shopify (Settings > Files) or Netlify (create manually: sitemap.xml).
  • Submit to Google Search Console (search.google.com, Sitemaps).
  • Run Screaming Frog (free) to find broken links, 404 errors, or redirect loops. Fix in Shopify (edit URLs) or Netlify (update HTML).
  • Test load time on PageSpeed Insights (<2 seconds, 90+/100). Compress images (WebP format) and minify CSS/JavaScript.
  • Test mobile-friendliness (mobile-friendly.test). Use responsive CSS (@media queries).
  • Add product schema to sales pages.

4.3 Keyword Research

  • Sign up for SEMrush (free trial) or Google Keyword Planner (ads.google.com).
  • Find 10 primary keywords (1k–10k monthly searches, low difficulty, e.g., “wellness e-commerce marketing”) and 20 long-tail (100–1k searches, e.g., “scale yoga gear business”).
  • Analyze competitors (e.g., Goop) in SEMrush for keyword gaps.
  • Save in Google Sheets (KPI Tracker).

6. Google Ads

6.1 Why Google Ads?

Google Ads drive immediate traffic for high-intent keywords, complementing SEO. Expect 4:1 ROAS with optimization.

6.2 Campaign Setup

  • Create a Google Ads account (ads.google.com) and link to Google Analytics.
  • Set up 3 campaigns: Search (keywords like “wellness marketing playbook”), Shopping (product feeds), Remarketing (site visitors).
  • Create ad groups by product categories (e.g., supplements, yoga gear).
  • Start with $150/month ($35/week) of your $500–$1,000 budget.

6.3 Ad Creation

  • Write 3 headlines per ad (e.g., “Scale Your Wellness Brand,” “High-ROI Playbook”).
  • Use dynamic keyword insertion (e.g., {KeyWord:Supplements}).
  • Add site links (e.g., “Shop Now,” “Free Guide”).
  • Upload product feeds for Shopping ads via Shopify (Apps > Google Shopping).

6.4 Optimization

  • Install conversion tracking (Google Ads > Tools > Conversions).
  • A/B test headlines and descriptions weekly.
  • Pause ads with <1% CTR or >$20 cost-per-conversion.
  • Scale >4:1 ROAS ads by 20% weekly (e.g., $35 → $42).
  • Monitor ROAS daily in Google Ads to stay within 10–15% revenue cap.

Tip: Adjust ad copy for your niche (e.g., “Vegan Yoga Mats” for yoga gear).
